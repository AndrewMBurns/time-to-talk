Figuring out when to talk is half the battle. Scroll to see what we should look out for.

Before you start, remember that keeping our children safe online is easier if we are always looking, a little bit every day. Don't worry! You haven't left it too late.

Let's look out for the questions they're asking, or not asking.

Asking lots of questions.
One of the most obvious signs that we need to talk to our children about online is that they're asking questions about it. Maybe they've asked for their first phone, or asked about something they saw online at a friends house.
Asking no questions.
If our children aren't asking us questions, they could be asking the Internet. It's normal for children to feel embarassed about questions, so we can't assume that if they aren't asking they aren't wondering.

Let's take note of how they spend their time.

Time alone.
They might be spending more time online if they are alone a lot. Using technology should be something they feel happy doing in front of us, not just in private. It's important to enjoy the Internet together.
Time indoors.
Everyone needs time indoors but our children might be spending too much time at a screen if they are. Keeping an eye on this, and encouraging them to go outside can be super helpful.

Check out what their friends are up to, and what they do together.

Their friends are online.
If their friends are online, there's a good chance they are too. Seeing how their friends use social media, devices, games and apps can be a great clue as to what our children are doing.
Time on screen.
This might seem obvious, but we are all guilty of overlooking it. It all adds up. Thirty minutes can quickly become two hours! If your childs new favourite activity is staring at a screen, it might be a good time to talk about it.

If you've noticed any of these, it's time to have a chat. Before that, let's learn about it.
