Knowing the basics about online safety is better than nothing! Don't get overwhelmed with the details. We just need to learn enough to be able to chat about it with our children.

What do we need to know about devices?

First Smartphone.
68% of 8-13 year olds own their own smart phone. So, itâ€™s totally normal for younger children to ask for a device, we just need to do our own research accordingly and figure out what's best for our child.
Finding the settings.
They may look very different but usually, we can get to the settings on any device the same way. Look for an icon of a cog or search "location" and "privacy" to access them.

Okay, so what's the deal with apps?

Privacy settings.
Public and private profiles let us decide who sees what we post online. Best practice is to make sure our children's profiles are always private, and that any social apps they use we have tried and tested first.
Location settings.
Location settings keep our location secret. Children are safer not posting their location publicly. Location can be switched off on all devices in settings, and on an app to app basis.

What's the story with screentime?

Bad screentime.
There's a difference between good and bad screentime. Bad screentime is excessive gaming or phones at the dinner table. We need to make sure our children aren't spending hours online without realising.
Good screentime.
On the other hand, good screentime could be Skyping Granny or playing games online together. Establish rules, for us and for them. Respect those rules and everyone's happy.

We've got the basics. Class dismissed! Now, it's time to talk about it.
